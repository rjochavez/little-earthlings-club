" For A Healthier Child and The Planet "

Objective

At Little Earthlings Club we provide unique activities for children age 6 to 10 years old where they can enjoy playing while learning in a natural setting. Child exposure to engaging activities encourages healthy child development – physically and mentally. They are able to improve their motor skills and develop critical thinking as activities are designed to better observational skills and awareness.

Healthy Child, Healthy Planet

Why play and learn?


Playing outdoors is grounded on strong bodies of research. Childhood is a stage of developing mental processes, logic, emotions and self-awareness. Playing promotes physical, emotional, and mental wellbeing. With quality activities, they can experiment and explore solving problems. They are also given the opportunity to think creatively and cooperate with others.


Academic learning may put pressure on completing tasks but interactive learning methods provide freedom and healthy growth.

Why use nature?


Nature provides sustainable resources. They appeal to the senses of human beings – touch, sight, smell, taste, sound – all of which are stimuli that we constantly process through adulthood. Concepts related to mathematics, science and language are discoverable in nature thus helping to improve cognitive function. Studies also shows exposure to healthy microbes boosts immunity. Occupying natural space also reduces Attention Deficit Disorder (ADD) symptoms.

The Problem Gap


Few of the problems in optimizing healthy development of children can be linked to lack of spaces for play, parks, and congested outdoors. While schools facilitate learning, engaging activity settings to motivate their exploration and active learning are not available to many.

Our Mission


Promote learning and healthy development of children through active engagement with other people, with other life organisms, and with broader natural environment.
